Background and Rationale

Since 2022, the Consulate General of Uganda in Mombasa, working with key tourism stakeholders in Uganda and the Kenya Coast, has been promoting cooperation and complementarily between Uganda and Kenya through the Uganda- Kenya Coast partnership. The main aim of the partnership is to increase the number of tourists visiting Uganda and Kenya respectively through cross- selling of the tourism products, given that the two destinations possess different and unique tourism products that can complement rather than compete with one another. The partnership is also intended to promote growth of regional tourism.

The number of tourists visiting both destinations has been steadily growing, mainly driven by initiatives undertaken by both countries. According to the Annual Tourism Sector Performance Report 2024 by the Ministry of Tourism and Wildlife of Kenya, 225,559 Ugandans visited Kenya, making Uganda the number two source market for Kenya after USA. Meanwhile 466,000 Kenyans visited Uganda according to the 2025 report published by the Ministry of Tourism, Wildlife and Antiquities of the Republic of Uganda, making Kenya the number one source market for Uganda. These numbers point to the importance of regional source markets. The numbers can grow even further through continuous collaboration under the Uganda- Kenya Coast partnership and other countries in the region.

The partnership between the two destinations has been promoted courtesy of the annual Uganda-Kenya Coast tourism conferences and Fam trips, now in the fourth edition. Participation in the conferences has been growing from about 250 delegates in 2022 to over 600 delegates in 2024. The Fam trips have been organized with the aim of enabling the tourism sector players, especially tour operators, travel agents and hoteliers to have first-hand experiences of the tourism products in both destinations for competitive service delivery. In addition, since 2024, the Consulate on-boarded golf, with the aim of promoting golf tourism between Uganda and Kenya. The first golf tournament was held on 20th April 2024 at Vipingo Ridge, during the Uganda Festival, while the second tournament was held on 9th November 2024 in Kampala. The intention is to make the golf tournaments part of the activities of the annual tourism conferences. Ultimately, more sports activities will be brought on board, with the intention of promoting sports tourism.

The Conferences, the fam trips and the golf tournaments have led to the following achievements, among others:

  • Increased knowledge and awareness about the two destinations and the tourism products that they offer.

  • Increased number of visitors to both destinations.

  • Partnerships have been created.

  • Synergies between key tourism players from Uganda and Kenyan region have been built and enhanced.

  • Opportunities in joint marketing of the tourism products of Uganda and Kenya Coast have been promoted.

  • Platform for B2B networking, learning and information sharing provided.

  • Key tourism players from Uganda and the Kenya Coast region have been equipped with first- hand experience of the key tourism attractions to enable them sell the two destinations.

  • Entebbe-Mombasa route operated by Uganda Airlines has been promoted and popularized.

Against the above background, the Consulate General and key tourism stakeholders in Uganda and Kenya are organizing the 4th Uganda-Kenya Coast tourism conference and Fam trip. The conference will be held on 27th – 28th October 2025 at the Ocean Beach Resort & Spa, Malindi, Kilifi County. The conference will be preceded by a Fam trip to the Kenya Coast by Ugandan tourism stakeholders from 22nd – 26th October, and a golf tournament that will be held at Vipingo Ridge on 25th October 2025. The conference and related activities will leverage on the achievements of the previous years and the emerging issues in the partnership.

The conference will be held under the theme: “Leveraging innovation and technology to shape a Responsive and Inclusive Tourism: Youth and technology.”

As the tourism sector evolves, innovation and technology have emerged as key elements in shaping the future of tourism. Young people, with fresh ideas and tech-savviness, have important roles to play both as travelers and service providers in the tourism industry as tour operators, travel agents and hoteliers. They are, therefore, uniquely positioned to drive innovation in both tourism and hospitality. Through the use of digital tools, data-driven solutions and creative entrepreneurship, youth are contributing to a more responsive, inclusive and sustainable tourism industry.

Young people possess a natural affinity for emerging technologies and are often at the forefront of creating and adapting to new technology solutions that transform the way tourism and hospitality services are delivered. From developing mobile apps for seamless bookings, to designing virtual reality travel experiences, to using AI and data analytics for personalized customer service, the youth are introducing dynamic innovations that enhance efficiency, sustainability and customer satisfaction in the industry. Their creativity and entrepreneurial mindset have also led to the rise of tech-enabled startups in travel, sustainable tourism initiatives, and smart hospitality concepts. These innovations not only improve the visitor experience but also contribute to environmental conservation, cultural preservation and economic empowerment.

Moreover, young professionals and students are increasingly involved in research, digital marketing strategies and social media engagement, helping destinations and businesses reach global audiences in real-time and stay relevant in a competitive market.

The 4th Uganda- Kenya Coast Tourism Conference will, therefore, provide an opportunity to discuss how to build synergies around innovation and technology, with a focus on the Tech based Tourism.

Objectives

Objectives

The overall objective of the 4th Uganda- Kenya will be to have a conversation on how stakeholders in the tourism industry can tap into the emerging innovations and technology to drive tourism growth.

The specific objectives are:

  • To discuss and create awareness about the latest innovations and technologies in tourism promotion.

  • To identify and address the challenges in making use of innovations and technology in tourism promotion.

  • To discuss technology and innovation solutions that can contribute to sustainable tourism.

  • To address barriers and challenges to tourism between Uganda and Kenya.

  • To explore investment opportunities in the tourism sector.

  • To assess the progress made in the cooperation between Uganda and Kenya Coast since 2022.

  • To discuss a strategy for joint promotion between Uganda and Kenya through innovation and technology.

  • To build and consolidate more partnerships between tourism players from Uganda and Kenya.

  • To provide a platform for B2B networking, learning and information sharing.

  • To equip the key tourism players from Uganda and the Kenya coast with first-hand experiences of the key tourism attractions so that they are in better position to market these attractions.

  • To enhance connectivity between the two destinations.

  • To promote golf as a game that can grow the tourist arrivals in the two destinations.

The event Structure and Methodology

The Conference will be interactive, with inspiring industry leaders in technology and innovation, presentations and B2B which will provide a platform for networking, learning and sharing, equip the key tourism players.

There will be an exhibition by tourism stakeholders from Uganda and the Kenya Coast.

The conference will also include visits to tourism sites in Kenya.

The familiarization trip to Uganda will be planned and organized by the Uganda Consulate General in Mombasa, in conjunction with UTB, AUTO, PSFU, UHOA and key players in the tourism sector in Uganda.
